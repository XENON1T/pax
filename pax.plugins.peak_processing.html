pax.plugins.peak_processing.BasicProperties.compute_area_deciles(w)[source]¶

Return (index of mid area, array of the 0th ... 10 th area decile ranges in samples) of w e.g. range_area_decile[5] = range of 50% area = distance (in samples) between point of 25% area and 75% area (with boundary samples added fractionally). First element (0) of array is always zero, last element (10) is the length of w in samples.

pax.plugins.peak_processing.BasicProperties.integrate_until_fraction(w, fractions_desired, results)[source]¶

For array of fractions_desired, integrate w until fraction of area is reached, place sample index in results Will add last sample needed fractionally. eg. if you want 25% and a sample takes you from 20% to 30%, 0.5 will be added. Assumes fractions_desired is sorted and all in [0, 1]!

pax.plugins.peak_processing.BasicProperties.put_w_in_center_of_field(w, field, center_index)[source]¶

Stores (part of) the array w in a fixed length array field, with center_index in field’s center. Assumes field has odd length.

class pax.plugins.peak_processing.RejectNoiseHits.RejectNoiseHits(config_values, processor)[source]¶

Bases: pax.plugin.TransformPlugin

Remove hits in channels with many lone hits from peaks without many other hits. Works with a penalty point system (see #126): * The more lone hits you see in a channel, the more points a channel gets. * Channels with more than a (fairly low) number of penalty points are called suspicious.

For these, we’ll test every peak as follows: * A peak scores points for every pe in non-suspicious channels. * Hits in channels with more penalty points than the peak’s score are removed from the peak.
